Google WTM Tech Unicorn

Google WTM Tech Unicorn

Google's Women Techmakers Program Members

Thriving within limitation

Women Techmakers is a Google program that provides visibility, community, and resources for women in technology.

The audience: International women technologists ages 16-65 who are members of Women Techmakers and will be attending Google I/O 2016. They need something uniquely theirs for visibility and a sense of community amidst a predominantly male industry, let alone a conference of 7000 that is 77% male.

The design had to adhere to three guidelines: that of Google Marketing, that of this audience's identity and pain points, and that of print design. The result is a wordmark that resonates so strongly with the group, that the unicorn has been adopted into their internal culture.

Takeaways:
Limitations can inspire creativity.
ASCII art is challenging for silkscreening.
Eliminate need for nametag stickers at tech networking events.
Professional introverts enjoy t-shirts that double as icebreakers.
Anticipate special needs of users outside the US.
Provide just enough options. Too many causes choice paralysis for users.
